FastTween v0.1.0 - JMH Benchmark Release

This release introduces a rigorous Java Microbenchmark Harness (JMH) testing suite to the FastTween core. The new benchmark proves the extreme efficiency of the zero-allocation pooling architecture and raw primitive math interpolation on the JVM.

BENCHMARK HIGHLIGHTS
Measured on Windows 11, Intel Core i5-1135G7 (Surface Pro 8), JDK 25.0.1.

The Zero-Allocation Hotpath:
- Pooled Tween Update: over 36,000 operations per second (~36 ops/ms).
- By utilizing TweenPool (FastTweenOpt), the engine completely bypasses lambda-capturing, autoboxing, and object allocations. This guarantees absolutely zero Garbage Collector micro-stutters during high-FPS game loops.

Raw Primitive Throughput:
- Raw Math (Lerp/Easing): over 2.3 Billion operations per second (~2,356,842 ops/ms).
- Showcases the blazing speed of raw primitive floating-point math when TLAB and GC overhead are entirely removed from the equation.

The Allocation Myth (Creation vs Update):
- Standard Tween Creation: ~163,942 ops/ms
- Pooled Tween Creation: ~60,528 ops/ms
- The benchmark highlights a classic JVM optimization: due to highly optimized TLABs (Thread Local Allocation Buffers), creating a standard new Tween() is initially faster than searching a ThreadLocal object pool. However, standard creation inevitably triggers GC pauses over time, proving that pooling remains the superior choice for sustained zero-latency applications.

CHANGES:
- Added: fasttween-benchmark module fully integrated with JMH.
- Added: TweenBenchmark.java to stress test creation, update, and raw math routines.
- Improved: Replaced legacy measurements with rigorous JMH Blackholes and Forking isolation to eliminate JIT dead-code elimination artifacts.
- Improved: Pure ASCII terminal output to ensure maximum compatibility with Windows cmd.exe.
